Description: Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere, which means its surface temperatures can vary dramatically. Radius: 2,439.7 km Distance from Sun: 57.91 million km Orbit Period: 88 Earth days

Venus

Description: Venus is similar in size and structure to Earth, but it has a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in the solar system. Radius: 6,051.8 km Distance from Sun: 108.2 million km Orbit Period: 225 Earth days

Earth

Description: Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has a perfect combination of water, atmosphere, and temperature for life to thrive. Radius: 6,371 km Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km Orbit Period: 365.25 Earth days

Mars

Description: Mars is known as the Red Planet due to its reddish appearance. Scientists are exploring Mars for signs of ancient life and possibilities for future human missions. Radius: 3,389.5 km Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km Orbit Period: 687 Earth days

Jupiter

Description: Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system. It has a giant storm, known as the Great Red Spot, which has been raging for centuries. Radius: 69,911 km Distance from Sun: 778.5 million km Orbit Period: 12 Earth years

Saturn

Description: Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system, made up of ice and rock. It’s the second-largest planet in the solar system. Radius: 58,232 km Distance from Sun: 1.429 billion km Orbit Period: 29.5 Earth years

Uranus

Description: Uranus is an ice giant with a pale blue color due to the methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, which makes its seasons very extreme. Radius: 25,362 km Distance from Sun: 2.871 billion km Orbit Period: 84 Earth years

Neptune

Description: Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun. It has strong winds, and its blue color is due to the methane in its atmosphere. Radius: 24,622 km Distance from Sun: 4.495 billion km Orbit Period: 165 Earth years
